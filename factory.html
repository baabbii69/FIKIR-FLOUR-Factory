Inside the Mill

How We Mill

The Milling Process

From raw grain to finished flour — a 14-stage process controlled at every point.

Grain Receiving & Testing

Every truck of wheat arriving at our facility undergoes an automated sampling and testing procedure before it is accepted. Our in-line NIR (Near-Infrared) analysers provide instant protein, moisture, and falling number data within minutes of arrival. Only grain meeting our specification is accepted into storage.

  • Automated NIR protein and moisture analysis
  • Falling number assessment for enzymatic activity
  • Mycotoxin screening per EC regulation standards
  • Segregated silo storage by variety and protein grade

Multi-Stage Grain Cleaning

Our grain passes through a seven-stage cleaning system that removes stones, sand, foreign seeds, dust, and light impurities. Magnetic separators remove any metallic particles. The result is the cleanest possible grain before milling begins — a critical food safety foundation.

Tempering & Conditioning

Water is precisely added to bring the grain to the optimal moisture level for milling. The tempered grain rests for 18–24 hours in our conditioning bins, allowing moisture to penetrate the kernel evenly. Correct conditioning is essential for efficient separation of bran and endosperm during milling.

Roller Milling

Conditioned grain passes through a series of corrugated and smooth roller mills that progressively break down the kernel. Each pass separates flour of different granulometry and ash content. The system includes 14 roller stands and 28 sieve sections. The result is flour streams of precisely controlled quality characteristics.

Quality Control & Packaging

Every flour stream is continuously sampled and analysed. Our in-line moisture meters and protein analysers provide real-time data. Flour is then blended to achieve precise target specifications before being conveyed to our automated bagging and bulk loading systems. Final product is held in quarantine until lab release is confirmed.
